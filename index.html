About “Screams”

There are about forty “Screams” in total. Four of them are pictorial paintings (they appeared in the period between 1893 and 1910), the rest of the works are graphic (including printed graphics and drawings). The picture was conceived as part of the "frieze" - a series about love, life and death.

Watercolor painting lesson

European watercolor When paper appeared in Europe, watercolor painting appeared. Although this technique was used by such a master of the Renaissance period as a watercolor, it did not receive proper development. In the middle of the XVIII century, it was generally considered the lot of non-professionals.

The heyday of watercolor painting in Europe fell on the turn of the XVIII — XIX centuries. Watercolor painting was especially popular in Great Britain, where in 1804 the Society of Watercolourists was created. By the way, in English "watercolor" - water colors, water colors. It was in England that the main techniques of European watercolor painting were developed.
Among the most famous European watercolorists are Thomas Gertin, Joseph Turner, Paul Delaroche, Eugene Delacroix, Austrians Rudolf von Alt, Karl Schindler, Thomas Ender. In the US, watercolor glorified William Trost Richards, Thomas Moran, Winslow Homer.
